This is a doctoral dissertation project which will apply advanced network
techniques to interorganizational networks in order toreveal the process by
which new biotechnology firms gainlegitimacy, develop trust relations, and
reduce their resourcedependence on their environment. The study will use a
longitudinal data base of interorganizational exchanges between 1976-1990. The
biotechnology industry provides a unique case forstudying the networks between
organizations of several types. It will improve our understanding of how
networks affect the development of new ideas and products.
