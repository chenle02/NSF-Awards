The PI is hosting and helping to organize the 2008 Cyber Trust PI meeting at
Yale University, March 16-18 2008. Approximately, 300 attendees from the PI
community, NSF PMs, PMs from other Government agencies, representatives from the
U.S. industry research community, selected international visitors are expected.
This annual event serves:&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;? The PI community by giving them
the opportunity to present their research results through posters. Also, the PIs
are given the unique opportunity to learn about NSF programs other than Cyber
Trust and about programs in other Government agencies and in U.S. industry that
afford funding opportunities in Cyber Trust-related research.&lt;br/&gt;? Other
Government agencies by informing them of the exciting research ongoing in the
Cyber Trust program.&lt;br/&gt;? Similarly, U.S. industry, by alerting it of
exciting research results.&lt;br/&gt;? NSF, primarily through breakout sessions,
by discussions on future research challenges that will influence future
solicitations&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;The two days of the meeting
include:&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;? A panel on future directions for
cryptography.&lt;br/&gt;? Sessions on privacy (both future research directions
and industry needs), research programs of other Government agencies, research
programs in leading U.S. companies that bear on Cyber Trust, International
opportunities (with talks by program managers from leading European funding
agencies, the new CNCI multi-agency initiative on a bold new program in
security&lt;br/&gt;? Breakout sessions on research challenges dealing with:
privacy, security and privacy foundations, testbeds, the possibility of a
security grand challenge competition, towards an architecture for a future
Internet that provides much improved security and privacy over the current
Internet.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;
