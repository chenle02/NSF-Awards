Under the Academic Research Infrastructure Program the Principal Investigators
have submitted a request that consists of three parts: (1) centralized four
processor computer and data server; (2) computer-based teaching and research
laboratory; (3) distributed workstations to support software development. This
system will be utilized for both multidisciplinary research and education in the
fields of hydrology, botany, microbiology, geography, civil engineering,
environmental science, computer science and zoology.***
