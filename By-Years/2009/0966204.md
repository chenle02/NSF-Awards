This project provides scholarships for 36 STEM bachelor degree students, with
recruitment focused specifically on underrepresented groups. The targeted
disciplines are Biology, CIS, and Engineering Technology. The goals of the
program are to 1) develop and emphasize new science, technology, and
professional programs, 2) establish campus to community outreach, 3) increase
the level of need and merit based financial aid, 4) develop First Year
Programming to integrate academic enrichment and co-curricular activities, 5)
increase community engagement activities, 6) create a collegial and supportive
community, and 7) build partnerships with other academic institutions, community
organizations, and businesses.
