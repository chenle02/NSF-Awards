This grant provides funding for a workshop focusing on emerging technologies
related to a Wireless Mobile Internet. The workshop is a collaborative effort
between the Anderson School of Management, Computer Science, and the Electrical
and Mechanical Engineering Departments of UCLA. The workshop provides a forum
for academics, industry, and research establishments to target major aspects and
problems related to IP-based mobile Internet and wireless access to the
Internet. Technologies such as, WiFi, Bluetooth, 3G and Java are discussed and
the possibilities of their integration explored, with applications to mobile
commerce, wireless transactions, etc. The workshop takes the form of lectures,
and panel discussions by leaders in industry and academia. Lectures and
discussion transcripts will be made available to the public via the WEB. The
workshop is held on November 8-9, 2001 on the Campus of UCLA. The PI anticipates
that this will become an annual event.
