The "Virtual Zooarchaeology of the Arctic Project (VZAP)" has been funded as a
pilot project to create high definition digital images of a sample of
vertebrates found in the Arctic and make them available virtually via the web to
a wide audience of users both professional academics and K-12 educators. In
addition, the project funds a stakehoder workshop, including a broad cross
section of the professional zooarchaeology community and educators to assist in
in the development and evaluation of the VZAP technology and to facilitate its
potential use.
