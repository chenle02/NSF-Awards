This workshop has been organized to bring together representatives from
universities, electric utilities, industry, the military and government to
address national needs in power electronics education and research. The
objectives of the workshop will include: assessment of national manpower needs
in power electronics through the year 2000 and beyond; review of the present
status of power electronics education and research in the U.S. and abroad;
review of university/industry/government relations in the U.S. and abroad with
respect to research support, patent issues, publication issues, technology
transfer and commercialization, job opportunities in power electronics; review
university needs in establishing power electronic programs; and review of the
mechanisms by which utilities, industry, government and the military can work
with universities to meet national needs in power electronics education and
research.
