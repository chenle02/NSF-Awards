This award is to hold a conference focusing on how the Great Depression has
affected the conduct of public policy. In particular, the conference will offer
papers on the impact of the Great Depression on trends in the conduct of
monetary policy, fiscal stabilization policy, the relative magnitude of state
and local versus federal spending, government involvement in insuring and
regulating labor markets, and international economic relations. The conference
papers will be published as conference volume which will be of interest both to
academic researchers as well policy makers interested in economic policy
matters.
