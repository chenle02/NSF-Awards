&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;The University of Puerto Rico's UPR goals are to meet
Puerto Rico's PR demands for excellent, highly trained science and technology
S&amp;T professionals, and assist in meeting the nation's need for diverse
S&amp;T perspectives. To those ends, UPR is being funded through NSF's AGEP
program to attain the following:&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Increase the average
number of STEM Ph.D.'s to 40 per year&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Increase enrollment
in STEM Ph.D. programs by 25%&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Increase the number of
stateside students in UPR STEM Ph.D. programs by
22&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Increase the number of women in engineering and chemical
physics by 10&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Increase the number of STEM Ph.D. graduates
opting for academic careers by 15&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Institutionalize PR-
AGEP's policies and practices. &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;To attain the objectives,
UPR will build on PR-AGEP Phase I successes by doing the
following:&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Enhancing PR-AGEP Phase I
"bridging"&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;TA, scholarly productivity, and peer mentoring
workshops, &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Continuing the recruitment of minority and
female students by systematically working with PR and Mainland U.S. REU
programs, &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Establishing a centralized tracking form to
follow the progress of each graduate student, &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Providing
twenty awards of $20,000 each year to attract talented students into academia
and areas with a particular shortage, and &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Completing the
institutionalization of workshops, the tracking system, and recruitment
guidelines.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;
