9452990 Stombler The Georgia Institute for Technology seeks partial funding from
NSF to host the Science Olympiad National Tournament on May 17-18, 1996. The
Science Olympiad is a national pre-college program to encourage interest in and
study of mathematics, science and technology. It is based on the athletic model
that includes practice, teamwork, competition and recognition. Science Olympiad
has activities designed for grades K-12. Each year, 100 state champion teams
from across the nation compete in a National Tournament comprised of 32 science
and technology events. Competition is at two levels: middle school (grades 6-9)
and secondary school (grades 9-12). ***
