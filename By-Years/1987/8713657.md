The Lick Observatory of the University of California, Santa Cruz will host the
International Astronomical Union (IAU) Symposium No. 134 on "Active Galactic
Nuclei" from August 15 to August 19, 1988. The Symposium will take place on the
Santa Cruz campus of the University of California and will involve approximately
200 participants from institutions all over the world. The Symposium will
provide an opportunity to bring together Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) research
workers from all over the world to exchange ideas and results on this very
important topic. active Galactic Nuclei are quasars, Seyfert galaxies, and other
extragalactic objects with highly energetic nuclei. Support will be provided for
travel and living expenses of some of the participants. IAU and the University
of California, Santa Cruz are also providing financial support.
